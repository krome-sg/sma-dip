1. Social Media Analytics
Conducted by Institute of Systems Science, National University of Singapore (NUS-ISS)
This module focuses on analysing social media data using tools and best practices. Through understanding of what customers think about your organisation, products and services, you can leverage the insights and analytics to empower your organisation to make intelligent business decisions. Topics covered include digital marketing concepts, social media analytics, social media listening, paid social, reporting and KPIs.
At the end of the module, participants will be able to

Analyse digital marketing campaign schedule and determine factors affecting its implementation
Evaluate the different social media platforms and their associated metrics
Review and evaluate the effectiveness of social media campaigns for different platforms
Use and apply social media listening and monitoring tools to gather data
Formulate effective actions based on the analytics
2. Web Analytics & SEO
Conducted by Institute of Systems Science, National University of Singapore (NUS-ISS)
This module focuses on web analytics principles, tools and applications that will help organisations to track and optimise website effectiveness. Participants will learn to implement and apply web analytics for business decision making. Topics covered include web analytics fundamentals, tools, and strategies. There will also be a hands-on session with Google Analytics features, reporting and applications. Other topics include web improvement strategies, SEO and other related tools.
At the end of the module, participants will be able to

Identify the sales and marketing information required by users of the information
Understand relevant web analytics tools and make recommendations to their organisation
Generate, analyse and modify required traffic reports according to information requirements and needs of sales and marketing functions
Implement and apply web analytics and metrics to improve web performance
Use Google Analytics tool as an example to monitor website performance, understand users and their behaviour, optimise content, optimise search engine performance (SEO) and assess campaign performance
3. Digital Marketing
Conducted by Association of Advertising and Marketing Singapore (AAMS)
This module focuses on the holistic understanding of digital marketing – from concepts to creation to implementation, tracking and optimization. This course program will cover the most up-to-date digital marketing practices, tips, frameworks, and insights to help adult learners apply their learnings to their businesses and professional jobs.
At the end of the module, participants will be able to

Acquire the basic concepts, terminology, and appreciation around the domain of Digital Marketing
Establish a good foundation on the right path to further acquire knowledge and experience in the practice of Digital Marketing
Understand how to choose the right digital marketing channels to meet business objectives
Develop a strategic mindset that integrates digital marketing into their respective functions
4. Making Online Content
Conducted by Singapore Media Academy (SMA)
This module covers the basics in story structure, what makes a compelling story and how it can and has been applied to different mediums like film, television and stage plays. Covering the A to Z of making good and compelling content and/or short videos from concept, pre-production and production to post-production using consumer products, equipment and software like mobile phones and complimentary editing software.
At the end of the module, participants will be able to

Understand story structure, plot design and variants of the three-act structure
Develop storylines and plot structure for online content
Write basic narrative scripts for online content
Learn pre-production process for making online content
Make content for online purposes
Edit and finish online content

Assessment
Each module comes with specific individual and/or group assessments, which participants are required to complete successfully. Participants will need to be marked Competent for all the modules to be awarded the Professional Certificate of Digital Marketing and Content Creation.

The assessments can take the form of written quizzes, short questions and answers or group work presentations. Lecturers will advise accordingly in class. Participants will also need to complete a final group project presentation at the end of the programme.
Certification
Participants certified Competent for all the modules will be awarded the Professional Certificate of Digital Marketing and Content Creation.

Post-Secondary Education Account (PSEA)
The PSEA is administered by the Ministry of Education (MOE) and is open to all eligible Singapore Citizens. Students may use their or their siblings' PSEA funds to pay the course fees.
For payment of approved fees and charges, you can withdraw the PSEA funds by establishing a Standing Order with MOE or by submitting ad hoc withdrawal applications as and when withdrawals are to be made.
How to apply to use the funds
Account holders can apply to withdraw the PSEA funds for payment of approved fees and charges through one of these ways:

Establishing a Standing Order (SO)

This is a one-time application.
It will apply to all future withdrawals by the approved institution and will cease only when terminated or replaced.
Submit the completed PSEA Standing Order Form (361KB) to the institution.
If they are no longer studying in an approved institution, the SO can be terminated by submitting an Online Application using their SingPass.

Submitting an Ad Hoc Withdrawal Application

This is a one-time withdrawal for a specific purpose. Submit the completed PSEA Ad Hoc Withdrawal Form (200KB) to the institution for each withdrawal.
Please consult SMA recruitment staff on which form to use.
Please note that PSEA is not to be used to offset the administrative fees (e.g., course deferment, re-assessment fee, make up lesson, etc.) and any other miscellaneous fees.
More information on PSEA: https://www.moe.gov.sg/financial-matters/psea
