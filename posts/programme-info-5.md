Part-Time Study | Course Fee SubsidyDesigned to empower marketers with cutting-edge skills, this programme provides a comprehensive roadmap to navigate the Social Media landscape. Through a carefully crafted curriculum, participants will master the art of crafting persuasive copy for interactive media, creating captivating online content spanning diverse formats and social media platforms, and strategically leveraging social media platforms to promote products and services effectively.
Career Prospects
Digital Marketer, Social Media Marketer, Online Content Creator, Vlogger
Course Modules
1.Develop and Manage Strategic Plan for a Digital Media Product
Develop and Manage Strategic Plan for a Digital Media Product (Classroom and Synchronous E-Learning) 
A ‘Business School’ module that operationalises strategy. Participants will be equipped with the tools to help them analyse and understand the business and outcomes that their Digital Media Products are supposed to support; the audiences they need to engage with their Digital Media Products; and the media options available to them.

2.Write and Edit Copy for Interactive Media Projects
Write and Edit Copy for Interactive Media Projects (Classroom and Synchronous E-Learning) 
In this module, participants will learn to plan and write website content and craft effective copy on social media. In addition, techniques will be shared on conducting interviews for articles, creating story ideas for websites, and writing headlines that engage the audience. It will be conducted in the style of workshops for every session to have tangible results.

3.Making Online Content
Making Online Content (Classroom and Synchronous E-Learning) 
This module covers the basics in story structure, what makes a compelling story, and how it can and has been applied to different mediums like film, television, stage plays, and online social media platforms. Following this will be the A-to-Z of making good and compelling content and/or short videos from concept, pre-production, and production to post-production using entry level equipment and software like mobile phones and complimentary editing software. 

4.Promote Products and Services on Social Media
Promote Products and Services on Social Media (Classroom and Synchronous E-Learning) 
This module offers participants knowledge in social media fundamentals, where they will learn how to leverage social media to meet business objectives. Apart from gaining a comprehensive overview of social media, it also teaches participants ways of creating desirable and effective display content for social media. 

5.Social Media - Monitoring, Metrics and Analytics
Social Media - Monitoring, Metrics and Analytics (Classroom and Synchronous E-Learning) 
This module delves into harvesting essential data from social media platforms, strategies, and campaigns. It also dives into the details of the sources of the numbers, covering topics like social optimised videos, email marketing, eCommerce costings, and website building. These are the assets/places digital marketers need to monitor, analyse and optimise closely. 

6.Social Media - Public Relations, Ethics and Crisis Management
Social Media - Public Relations, Ethics and Crisis Management (Classroom and Synchronous E-Learning) 
This module offers participants knowledge of public relations fundamentals on social media. While social media has given customers the freedom to express and converse, it has allowed organisations to engage their customers directly and increased the risk of various social media crises. As a result, social media has changed not only some fundamental ways marketing and public relations should be conducted; it also affects how customers can and should be engaged, managed, and nurtured. 
Besides teaching the new marketing and public relations paradigm, this module also covers “brand image” in the Google universe. Participants will learn how brands can manage their search engine optimisation, search ads, and presence in various web/ video/ app platforms in the form of display ads.

7.Manage IP at a Strategic Level
Manage IP at a Strategic Level (Classroom and Synchronous E-Learning) 
Innovation and intangible assets are two known aspects driving the global economy. New media innovations cover diverse technology fields, including social media, business models, mobile commerce, metaverse, NFTs, and more. Within the media industry, creators often generate novel ideas and works, and they must be aware of the options available for the protection and commercialisation of their creative works. 
This module provides participants with an immersive learning experience through web-based tools to identify interesting global inventions and ideas related to new media and beyond. Using highly engaging and interactive learning activities, participants will gain the skills and knowledge that allow them to generate more effective new ideas; identify the ownership of various Intellectual Property (IP); and options for IP protection, including the use of blockchain technology and monetisation options for their IP.
Mode of Training
In-person and/or Online
Assessment

Participants will be assessed based on their assignments, class participation and class attendance.
Assessments may include role plays, quizzes, assignments, projects, and written examinations.
Participants need to achieve at least 75% attendance for each module before they are allowed to sit for the assessment.

Certification
Completion of all the required modules will qualify candidates for the Specialist Diploma in Social Media Marketing and Online Content Creation, issued by Singapore Media Academy.
 
Participants will also receive a Certificate of Completion issued by Singapore Media Academy for each module completed.
