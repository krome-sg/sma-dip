Course Modules
1. SIRS-Alibaba Taobao University: Boost Sales through Effective Content Marketing (Conducted in Mandarin) 
Conducted by Singapore Institute of Retail Studies (SIRS)
This module equips the professionals with the fundamental skills to embark a career in the Retail-Entertainment industry. It provides the know-how and deep skill learning in marketing products indirectly with entertainment element in the live broadcast format.
At the end of the course, participants will be able to

Understand the eco-system/ economy of Commerce/ Service/ Entertainment 
Apply the new content marketing, live broadcast trend, video production for today’s world 
Analyse the live broadcaster role, set team KPI and resource planning 
Evaluate the live broadcaster engagement techniques with Big Data analytics 
Building a system and management of Fans Community

2. Making Online Content (Conducted in English)
Conducted by Singapore Media Academy (SMA)
This module will cover the basics in story structure, what makes a compelling story and how it can and has been applied to different mediums like film, television and stage plays. Covering the A to Z of making good and compelling content and/ or short videos from concept, pre-production and production to post-production using entry-level equipment and software like mobile phones and complimentary editing software.
At the end of the course, participants will be able to

Understand story structure, plot design and variants of the three-act structure
Develop storylines and plot structure for online content
Write basic narrative scripts for online content
Learn pre-production process for making online content
Make content for online purposes
Edit and finish online content

Assessment
Each module comes with specific individual and/ or group assessments, which participants are required to complete successfully. Participants will need to be marked Competent for all the modules to be awarded the Professional Certificate in Digital Retail Entertainment.

The assessments can take the form of written quizzes, short questions and answers or group work presentations. Lecturers will advise accordingly in class. Participants will also need to complete a final group project presentation at the end of the programme.
Certification
Participants certified Competent for all the modules will be awarded the Professional Certificate in Digital Retail Entertainment.
Trainers
 
Zhang Zekun
     Zekun is a Certified lecturer at Taobao University. He specialises in the areas of content creation and distribution of short videos, especially those on ByteDance-owned platforms. He operates multiple Tiktok IPs, and has about 15 million followers. He provides services for many Tiktok influencers with millions of followers each. He is also a mentor at Onion Group, a top Tiktok-certified MCN.
Wang Minhui
      Minhui, a Certified Lecturer of Taobao University, has 13 years of experience in import and export trade, operation of three Tmall stores, as well as practical operational experience of several top-level stores. As the initiator of the Hand-in-Hand Global Business Alliance, he recorded monthly sales exceeding 50 million RMB during the VIP month. He has carried out training and education programs with multinational trade organisations, and his areas of expertise include content marketing, live broadcasting and online celebrity incubation.
Ma Mingyang
      Mingyang is a Certified Lecturer of Taobao University, as well as a CBN Data Advisor. He started business on Taobao in 2008 as an entrepreneur, and in 2011, he registered a company and opened a Tmall store. He has traded a variety of products including women’s shoes, women’s clothing, jewelry, healthcare products. His areas of expertise include content marketing, hit product development, as well as methods to attract traffic from external websites.
Pang Yoong Hui (Ray)
      Ray is an award-winning filmmaker, both locally and internationally.
He represented Singapore in the 1st World Event Young Artists (WEYA), organised alongside the London 2012 Olympics, a global art and culture event held in Nottingham, United Kingdom. He was also the recipient of the 'Ten Outstanding Young Persons of the World' in 2012 Singapore Merit Awardee by JCI TOYP. In 2012, Ray was nominated as the finalist for Yahoo Singapore 9 and runner up for Entertainment Category. During the same year, his short film "Closer To Me" has been shortlisted for the prestigious Louis Vuitton’s second edition Journey Awards, making him the only Singaporean nominated for the Jury's awards People's Choice Award of the contest. The film later gets to participate at the 66th Festival de Cannes's Short Film Corner. Ray was also invited to screen some of his films at the 8th Sapporo Short Fest 2013 under a special programme 'Asian Typhoon'. His short film 'The Grandfather' was the winner of 180 Short Film Competition 2013, under the Professional Category organised by Singapore Media Academy and supported by the Singapore Workforce Development Agency (WDA), now known as SkillsFuture Singapore (SSG)
